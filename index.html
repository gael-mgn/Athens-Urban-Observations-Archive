Athens Urban Observations Archive

An Online Ethnographic and Urban Repository on Athenian Spaces in Transition
— English & Greek —
Vision Objectives Scope Methods Website Architecture Ethics Outputs

1. Project Vision

The project aims to create a collaborative online archive documenting subtle, overlooked, or transitional spaces of Athens. It blends urban studies, ethnography, visual culture, and citizen contributions to build a living memory of the city’s informal, contested, or reappropriated places.

The platform serves as a digital observatory of everyday Athenian life, focusing on how inhabitants reinterpret, reclaim, or resist urban space.

2. Objectives

Urbanistic Objectives

  • Map urban voids, vacant lots, derelict spaces.
  • Understand how these spaces transform or are reused by communities.

Ethnographic Objectives

  • Record informal uses such as kiosks or micro-places of daily life.
  • Analyze symbolic markings and inscriptions as traces of identity and tension.
  • Capture stories and lived experiences around these spaces.

Visual & Cartographic Objectives

  • Integrate sketches, photography, and cartographic layers.
  • Create visual narratives of micro-geographies.

Participatory Objectives

  • Allow contributions via email: photos, texts, memories.
  • Build a collective memory of Athens’ hidden or marginal places.

3. Scope of Study

A. Urban Wastelands (Φρίχες / Friches)

  • Empty lots
  • Abandoned buildings
  • Suspended construction sites
  • Informal green spaces
Key questions: Who uses these spaces? How do they evolve? What do they reveal about community needs or land pressures?

B. Reappropriated Spaces

  • Squats and self-managed places
  • Community gardens
  • Informal meeting points
  • Reclaimed rooftops, stairways, shortcuts
Key questions: How do Athenians reclaim the urban fabric? What forms of autonomy or creativity emerge?

C. Micro-Spaces of Everyday Life

  • Kiosks, koulouri stands, street vendors
  • Benches, shaded corners, small squares
  • Local cafés and informal gathering spots
Key questions: How do micro-spaces shape social life? How do they resist gentrification?

D. Symbolic & Political Traces

  • Anti-tourist graffiti
  • Anarchist/political slogans
  • Neighborhood identity tags
  • Posters, stickers, signs
Key questions: What do these inscriptions reveal about conflict, identity, or resistance?

4. Methods & Data Collected

  • Field Notes & Observations: micro-descriptions, sensory notes.
  • Drawings & Sketches: architectural details, diagrams, perspectives.
  • Photographs: contemporary & archival.
  • Cartographies: interactive maps, GIS layers, temporal evolution.
  • Contributions by Email: stories, memories, multilingual submissions.

6. Tone & Scientific Anchoring

The project blends urban anthropology, critical urbanism, visual ethnography, and public humanities. It references thinkers such as Lefebvre, Ingold, de Certeau, and scholars of Greek urban studies.

7. Ethics & Anonymity

  • Respect for privacy and avoidance of identifiable personal data.
  • Contextualisation of sensitive or political content.
  • Clear statement on image rights and submissions.

8. Expected Outputs

  • An evolving online atlas of Athenian micro-urbanism.
  • A participatory multilingual platform.
  • A resource for students, artists, researchers, residents.
  • A long-term memory of Athens’ informal or resistant spaces.
